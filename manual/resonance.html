Resonance Range

reconstruct (cross section from resonance parameters)

   reconstruct X-min X-max delta-X
   reconstruct X
   reconstruct

DeCE has a capability to reconstruct cross sections from resolved resonance parameters, though the resonance formulae are limited to Multi-Level Breit-Wigner, or Reich-Moore. X-min is the lowest energy, X-max is the highest energy, and delta-X is the energy increment.

As in the second syntax, if delta-X is not given, cross section at a given energy is calculated.

When no argument is provided, DeCE determines the energy grid automatically, and gives the cross sections in the resonance region.

reconangdist (angular distribution from resonance parameters)

   reconangdist X-min X-max delta-X
   reconangdist X
   reconangdist

Elastic scattering angular distributions for given energy points are calculated from the resonance parameters. See reconstruct for the energy range parameters.

smoothangdist (energy averaged Legendre coefficients from resonance parameters)

   smoothangdist width

Produce smoothed (energy-averaged) Legendre coefficients for elastic scattering. The Legendre coefficients are calculated from the resolved resonance parameters at equi-distant energy points, then smoothed by a Gaussian weighting function, with the width of width.

resonanceangdist (merge energy averaged Legendre coefficients with existing elastic scattering angular distributions)

   resonanceangdist  npoint

Replace Legendre coefficients given in MF4/MT2 by the smoothed (energy-averaged) Legendre coefficients produced from resolved resonance parameters. This command replaces the coefficients inside the resonance region only, and it works when Legendre coefficients are MF4/MT2 are given (not tabulated angular distributions). Above the resonance region, existing coefficients will be kept. npoint is the number of energy points given inside the resonance region. This excludes the boundary energy point, so that the actual number will be +1. The energy-average is performed by the Gaussian smoothing with the energy width of the resonance region divided by npoint.
